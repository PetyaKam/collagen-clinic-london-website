What is Glycation?

Glycation is a key biomarker of skin aging, where sugar molecules attach to collagen and elastin fibers, causing them to become stiff and brittle. This process creates Advanced Glycation End-products (AGEs) that accumulate in your skin over time, accelerating the aging process and reducing skin elasticity.

Our non-invasive glycation analysis measures AGE accumulation in your skin, providing crucial insights into your skin's biological age and structural integrity. By measuring glycation, Dr. Zolman can identify the root causes of skin aging and develop targeted interventions to reduce AGE accumulation, restore skin elasticity, and reverse your skin's biological age.

Treatment Response

How Glycation Affects Your Skin

Understanding glycation is essential for effective anti-aging treatment:

The Glycation Process

Cause: Sugar molecules bind to collagen and elastin proteins

Effect: Creates stiff, cross-linked structures that cannot function properly

AGE Formation

Process: Glycated proteins form advanced glycation end-products

Impact: AGEs accumulate and cause progressive skin damage

Visible Signs

Symptoms: Loss of elasticity, sagging, wrinkles, and dull skin tone

Progression: Accelerates with high sugar intake, UV exposure, and time
