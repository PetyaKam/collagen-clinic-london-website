What is the Pigmentation Protocol?

The Pigmentation Protocol is Dr Zolman's evidence-based treatment approach specifically designed to address brown pigmentation, sun damage, and uneven skin tone. This comprehensive protocol combines Sunekos HA biostimulator with the world's smallest needles for minimal discomfort, advanced laser resurfacing, and botox long-acting for enhanced pigment removal.

This protocol targets pigmentation at multiple levels using LaseMD laser with drug delivery and botox long-acting for enhanced pigment removal. For patients requiring full-face and eye coverage or additional texture refinement, Tixel thermo-mechanical ablative (TMA) technology with drug delivery can be incorporated.

How the Pigmentation Protocol Works

The protocol combines three powerful treatments that work synergistically to address pigmentation from multiple angles:

Sunekos Treatment

Strengthens skin barrier function and improves overall skin health, making skin more resilient to pigmentation triggers

LaseMD Ultra Laser

Precision fractional laser treatment that targets melanin in pigmented areas, breaking down dark spots and stimulating skin renewal while delivering brightening actives

Botox Long-Acting

Enhances pigment removal pathways and helps prevent new pigmentation from forming

Optional upgrade: Tixel TMA technology with targeted drug delivery can be selected for full face and eye coverage when additional texture refinement or laxity improvement is required.

Who Can Benefit from the Pigmentation Protocol?

This protocol is ideal for individuals experiencing various forms of hyperpigmentation and uneven skin tone:

Sun Damage

Those with age spots, sun spots, and freckles caused by UV exposure

Melasma

Individuals with hormonal pigmentation, often triggered by pregnancy or birth control

Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation

Those with dark spots left behind after acne, injury, or other skin trauma

Uneven Skin Tone

Anyone seeking to achieve a more uniform, radiant complexion

Treatment Process

Initial Consultation

Comprehensive skin analysis to assess pigmentation type, depth, and contributing factors. Customised treatment plan development.

Treatment Sessions

3-4 sessions spaced 2-3 weeks apart. Each session includes Sunekos injections, LaseMD Ultra treatment (or Tixel upgrade), and Botox Long-Acting application.

Session Duration

Each treatment session lasts approximately 60-90 minutes, including preparation, treatment, and recovery time.

Home Care

Prescribed skincare routine including brightening actives and sun protection to maintain and enhance results.

Expected Results

Immediate Effects

Some improvement in skin texture and tone may be visible immediately after treatment

Weeks 2-4

Gradual lightening of pigmented areas as the body naturally eliminates treated melanin

Weeks 4-8

Significant reduction in pigmentation, improved skin tone uniformity, and enhanced skin radiance

Long-term Benefits

Maintained results with continued improvement in skin health and protection against future pigmentation

Pre-Treatment Preparation

  • Avoid sun exposure and use broad-spectrum sunscreen daily for 2 weeks before treatment
  • Discontinue use of retinoids and other active skincare products 1 week prior
  • Avoid blood-thinning medications and supplements for 1 week before treatment
  • Stay well-hydrated and maintain a healthy skincare routine
  • Discuss any medications, health conditions, or recent cosmetic procedures during consultation

Post-Treatment Care

  • Avoid sun exposure completely for 48 hours after treatment
  • Use broad-spectrum sunscreen (SPF 50+) daily and reapply every 2 hours when outdoors
  • Apply prescribed brightening serums as directed by your practitioner
  • Avoid picking or scratching treated areas
  • Use gentle, fragrance-free skincare products for the first week
  • Follow up appointments as scheduled for optimal results

Side Effects & Safety

Common Side Effects

Recovery Time

Safety Profile

All treatments are FDA-approved with excellent safety records when performed by qualified practitioners
